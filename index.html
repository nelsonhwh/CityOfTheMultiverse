City of the Multiverse

“City Of The Multiverse” is an immersive installation operating in "The Gallery 360" [1] omnidirectional visualisation system. Immersion is enhanced through stereoscopic projection, which creates a true-to-life experience of being inside a virtual city through viewing similar but different perceived views from the left and right eyes of the city at 1:1 scale. ARTIST STATEMENT

City of the Multiverse immerses visitors in the different layers of a virtual city, that is perhaps Hong Kong itself. The experience is constructed from abstract graphic forms, graphic words, representations of buildings and people, and the sounds and atmosphere of daily life. The sound of the streets, the voice of nature, the grand buildings, the busy crowds, the colors of the sky, and numerous instructions imparting information can all be found in a big city. However, everyone in the city observes and interprets them differently; some feel more while some feel less. And eventually, we tend to believe that what we observe by ourselves is the only way of seeing, and we do not take account of others’ point of view. This leads us to inhabit different multiverses even if we live in the same city. The city of the Multiverse places multiple phenomena directly in front of the visitor at the same time, to remind them that the city harbors different perspectives that we often fail to see. City of the Multiverse takes place in AVIE (2022)—a large 360-degree, panoramic, stereoscopic projection theatre, which offers a true-to-life experience of being inside a virtual city and viewing similar but different perceived views from the left and right eyes of the city at 1:1 scale. The city of the Multiverse creates an immersive, multi-perspectival, virtual city world through the stereoscopic experience of abstract and representational forms The choosing of the left and right perspective to capture the whole stereoscopic projection reminds us how the contemplation of our surroundings actually involves a process of filtering and selection which we do not normally reflect upon. Here we can choose how to observe and contemplate our surroundings with one or more than one perspective. The combination of both perspectives will lead people to a different point of view on the urban landscape. We can use each eye to see our city separately or use both eyes to get the whole picture of our city. We can choose what to see and choose not to see. The future will be made by what we observe and think. With the multi-layered, multimedia augmented-reality features, City of the Multiverse’s virtual, 1:1 scale 3-D visualization of the virtual city constructs an immersive embodied visual experience aesthetically which showcases different perspectives, values, and meanings of city life. It symbolizes how we might perceive our society in the future. More voices and points of view are reflected in this experience of the city which we perceive from different perspectives through voices, sounds, graphic words, and visuals. Through the trope of stereoscopic vision in an immersive cityscape it provides a way for us to imagine how we might better constitute our society and how to embrace different perspectives and points of view.
